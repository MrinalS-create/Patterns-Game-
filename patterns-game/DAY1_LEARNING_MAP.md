## 1. Marginal Thinking Framework

### Core Concept
**Start with BIGGER OUTCOME, then think about OUTPUT**

### From Your Notes
- "Start with BIGGER OUTCOME"
- "Then think about OUTPUT"
- Outcome first, then output

### Use Cases
1. **Team wants to build a feature**
   - ❌ Wrong: "Let's build a dashboard"
   - ✅ Right: "What outcome do we want? → Faster decision-making → Then: dashboard"

2. **Product planning**
   - ❌ Wrong: Start listing features
   - ✅ Right: Define business outcome first

### Interactive Challenge
**Question**: "Your team wants to add a new button. What should you ask first?"
- A) What color should it be?
- B) What outcome are we trying to achieve?
- C) Where should we put it?
- **Answer**: B

## 2. Problem Definition Framework

### Core Concept
**Good problems are: Usable, Feasible, Valuable**

### From Your Notes
- Usable - Can users use it?
- Feasible - Can IBM build it?
- Valuable - Does it provide value?

### Use Cases
1. **Evaluating an idea**
   - Check all three dimensions
   - If any is missing, it's not a good problem

2. **Prioritizing work**
   - Ideas that score high on all three = priority
   - Missing one dimension = needs more work

### Interactive Challenge
**Scenario**: "Idea: Build an AI that reads minds"
- Usable? Maybe
- Feasible? No (can't build it)
- Valuable? Maybe
**Question**: "Should we build this?"
- **Answer**: No - not feasible

**Text Box**: "Name the three dimensions of a good problem"

## 3. EOT (Enterprise Design Thinking) Framework

### Core Concept
**Human-Centered Loop: Observe → Reflect → Make**

### From Your Notes
- Focus on the root problem
- Loop: Observe / Reflect / Make
- Keys: Hills / Playbacks / Sponsor Users
- Making a step back

### The Three Principles
1. **Focus on user outcomes**
2. **Restless reinvention**
3. **Diverse, empowered teams**

### The Three Keys
1. **Hills**: Statements of intent as meaningful user outcomes
2. **Playbacks**: Regular check-ins to align the team
3. **Sponsor Users**: Real-world users who provide feedback

### Use Cases
1. **When requirements are unclear**
   - Observe: Talk to users, understand their world
   - Reflect: What's the real problem?
   - Make: Prototype and test

2. **When building new features**
   - Don't assume - observe first
   - Reflect on what you learned
   - Make something testable

### Interactive Challenge
**Question**: "User says 'I need a faster way to do X'. What's your first step?"
- A) Start designing the UI
- B) Observe how they currently do X
- C) Build a prototype
- **Answer**: B (Observe first!)

**Text Box**: "What are the 3 steps of the EDT loop?"
**Required**: Must include "observe", "reflect", "make"

## 4. PDLC (Product Development Lifecycle) Framework

### Core Concept
**Discovery → Delivery → Launch & Scale**

### From Your Notes
- Discovery: Build the right thing (from $20)
- Delivery: Build it right (customer value)
- Launch & Scale: Valuable

### The Three Phases

#### Discovery: Are we building the right thing?
- **Opportunity**: Is this a compelling market need?
- **Solution**: Can users get value from the solution?
- **Governing Docs**: Opportunity Assessment, PR-FAQ

#### Delivery: Are we building it right?
- **Design**: Does the design fulfill the functional need?
- **Build**: Is the experience instrumented?
- **Governing Docs**: Epic, User Story

#### Launch & Scale: Are customers getting value?
- **Launch**: Do we have product-market fit?
- **Governing Doc**: Launch Readiness

### Use Cases
1. **Starting a new project**
   - Begin in Discovery (not Delivery!)
   - Validate the opportunity first
   - Then design and build

2. **Project is failing**
   - Which phase are you in?
   - Did you skip Discovery?
   - Go back and validate

### Interactive Challenge
**Question**: "Your team starts coding immediately. What phase did they skip?"
- A) Delivery
- B) Discovery
- C) Launch
- **Answer**: B (Discovery!)

**Text Box**: "What are the 3 main phases of PDLC?"
**Required**: Must include "discovery", "delivery", "launch" or "scale"

## 5. Psychological Safety Framework

### Core Concept
**Biggest indicator of team performance**

### From Your Notes
- Psychological Safety = biggest indicator of team performance
- No trust = No healthy conflict
- Task vs Conflict Management

### What It Means
Team members can:
- Admit mistakes
- Say "I don't know"
- Ask for help
- Without fear of judgment

### Use Cases
1. **Building high-performing teams**
   - Create safety first
   - Then healthy conflict becomes possible
   - Performance follows

2. **When team isn't speaking up**
   - Lack of psychological safety
   - Need to build trust
   - Model vulnerability

### Interactive Challenge

## 6. Playbacks to Executives Framework

### Core Concept
**Get to the point quickly, explain WHY**

### From Your Notes
- Understand the situation / Read the room
- Preparation / Presentation / Storytelling
- Get the expectation right
- Explaining WHY is very important

### Key Steps
1. **Prepare**: Know your audience, do homework
2. **Get to the point**: Execs have limited time
3. **Explain WHY**: Why this matters, why now
4. **Know your ask**: Decision? Feedback? Resources?

### Use Cases
1. **5-minute exec meeting**
   - Lead with outcome
   - Explain why it matters
   - State your ask
   - Leave time for questions

2. **Playback presentation**
   - Set context first
   - Show the work
   - Explain decisions (WHY)
   - Get alignment

### Interactive Challenge
**Question**: "You have 5 minutes with an exec. What do you lead with?"
- A) Detailed process explanation
- B) The outcome and why it matters
- C) Your design portfolio
- **Answer**: B

**Text Box**: "What should you always explain in playbacks?"
**Required**: Must include "why"

## 9. Universal Experiences Framework

### Core Concept
**9-stage customer journey: Discover → Learn → Try → Buy → Onboard → Use → Get Help → Expand → End Use**

### From Day1_Summary.md
IBM's shared language for the complete customer lifecycle

### The 9 Stages

**Acquisition**:
1. **Discover**: Find solutions
2. **Learn**: Gain knowledge, evaluate
3. **Try**: Test through hands-on experience
4. **Buy**: Purchase to unlock access

**Retention**:
5. **Onboard**: Set up with minimal training
6. **Use**: Get work done
7. **Get Help**: Find answers, resolve issues

**Expansion**:
8. **Expand**: Grow solution, fulfill new needs
9. **End Use**: Discontinue or migrate

### Use Cases
1. **Designing new features**
   - Which stage does this serve?
   - Are we covering the full journey?
   - Where are the gaps?

2. **Improving product**
   - Map current experience to 9 stages
   - Find friction points
   - Prioritize improvements

### Interactive Challenge
**Question**: "User can't figure out how to use your product. Which stage needs work?"
- A) Discover
- B) Onboard
- C) Expand
- **Answer**: B (Onboard)

**Text Box**: "Name 3 of the 9 Universal Experience stages"
**Required**: Must type 3 stages
